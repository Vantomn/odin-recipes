Chicken Makhani (Indian Butter Chicken)

Chicken Makhani is an indian butter chicken recipe that features a full-flavored sauce that compliments the chicken perfectly and can be made as mild or as spicy as you want by adjusting the cayenne when cooking. This dish is also served with basmati rice and naan bread, so be aware that it is carb heavy, but if you can replace these two options if you want it to be slightly less in carbs with an alternative like quinoa.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of peanut oil
  • 1 shallot
  • 1/4 white onion
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 teaspoons of ginger garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons of lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons of garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup of tomato puree
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • 1/4 cup of plain yogurt
  • Salt and pepper for taste
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch

How to make Chicken Makhani:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large suacepan over medium-high heat. Saut√© shallot and onion ntil soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in butter, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, 1 teaspoon garam masala, chili powder, cumin, and bay leaf. Cook and stir for 1 minute. Add tomato sauce, and cook for 2 minutes continuing to frequently stir.
  3. Stir in half-and-half and yogurt. Reduce heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes, frequently stirring. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. Cook chicken until lightly browned, about 10 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat, and season with remaining 1 teaspoon garam masala and cayenne. Stir in a few spoonfuls of sauce, and simmer until liquid has reduced, and chicken is no longer pink. Add cooked chicken into sauce and stir together.
  6. Dissolve cornstarch into water, then mix into the sauce. Cook for 5 to 10 minutes.
